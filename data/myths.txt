Myth: Menstrual blood is dirty or impure. Fact: Menstrual blood is a natural mixture of blood and tissue and is not harmful.

Myth: People should not exercise during periods. Fact: Light exercise can reduce cramps and improve mood.

Myth: Pregnancy cannot happen during periods. Fact: Pregnancy is possible if ovulation happens early in the cycle.

Myth: Periods are only about bleeding. Fact: The menstrual cycle includes hormonal changes that affect energy, mood, and appetite.

Myth: Everyone with periods feels emotional or moody. Fact: Emotional changes vary from person to person.

Menstrual myths can cause fear, shame, and poor health practices.

Education and open discussion help break taboos and promote healthy menstrual behavior.
